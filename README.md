# 100Hires LinkedIn B2B Research

## Project overview

This repo is a structured research archive studying how real B2B practitioners, founders, operators, and consultants - use LinkedIn to grow their audience and influence organically. The goal is to study working examples, not theory, and extract patterns applicable to a B2B organic content strategy.

## Why I chose these experts

I selected 10 people based on one rule: **they had to practice what they post about.** Each person on this list either runs a company, advises clients directly, or has built measurable results (followers, revenue, or audience) using the exact methods they share. I deliberately avoided commentators and aggregators who repost others' frameworks without applying them.

Within that rule, the list ended up covering three angles that all feed into a B2B organic strategy:

- **AI and content tooling** - Ruben Hassid, Ishan Sharma, Jess Ramos, and Matt Barker post about how AI is changing content creation and workflows, which is increasingly part of how B2B creators produce at volume.
- **LinkedIn-specific organic growth and storytelling** - Saheli Chatterjee and Lara Acostar are studied directly for format, hooks, and storytelling structure on the platform itself.
- **B2B SaaS operators and GTM specialists** - Kyle Poyar, Jason Lemkin, and Aaron Levie represent the founder/operator voice, what experienced SaaS leaders actually say publicly, and how they use LinkedIn as a GTM and credibility channel.
- **Leadership and business frameworks** - Eric Partaker rounds out the set with a more executive-coaching angle, useful for studying how frameworks are visually packaged (heavy infographic use).

This mix was intentional: B2B organic strategy for founders isn't just about post formatting - it also depends on credibility (operators), distribution mechanics (growth specialists), and content production (AI/tooling). Studying all three angles together gives a fuller picture than any one alone.

## What I collected

- **10 experts** tracked in `research/sources.md`, each with LinkedIn profile, date added, practitioner rationale, focus areas, and a key insight about their content style.
- **5 recent LinkedIn posts per expert** (4 for one expert where only 4 were available at time of collection), saved in `research/linkedin-posts/`, organized one file per author.
- Each post entry includes the post date, and where available, the post text and format notes - not just links, so the content stays usable even if the original post is edited or removed later.

No YouTube transcripts or podcast content were collected for this project - LinkedIn posts only, by design, since the research question is specifically about LinkedIn organic strategy.

## Methodology notes

- Practitioner check: each expert had to be actively posting (not dormant) at the time of collection, and had to demonstrate hands-on application of what they talk about - not just commentary.
- Collection window: experts were added between **June 17–19, 2026**, and posts reflect each person's most recent activity as of their collection date.
- Annotations in `sources.md` are my own observations, written after reading each expert's recent posts - they're meant to capture *how* each person communicates, not just *what* they talk about.
