For a month, I watched my sister obsess over the Diet Coke shortage in India.
Every grocery run ended the same way:

"Did they have Diet Coke?"No.
"Check that store." No.
"Blinkit?" No.

At some point, I was annoyed:
Why can't she just... drink something else?
There are hundreds of beverages on the shelf.

- Regular Coke.
- Coke Zero.
- Pepsi.
- God sake...Sparkling water.

Surely they're all close enough? Apparently not.

In India, Diet Coke is marketed primarily as an identity.

It's associated with:

> Urban professionals
> Fitness-conscious consumers
> Creatives, founders, marketers, and young professionals
> Premium cafes and restaurants
> "Low-calorie lifestyle" signaling

The product is essentially selling:
"I care about health, but I still enjoy cola."

During the shortage, "Diet Coke parties" emerged in cities like Delhi and Mumbai. 

What's remarkable is that Diet Coke has developed a community that takes decades to build.

People:

- Post aesthetic Diet Coke photos
- Carry cans to work, feature it in desk setup
- Joke about addiction

But that's just me. Why do you think Indians are obsessed with Diet Coke?
